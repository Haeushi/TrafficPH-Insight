The Skyway Extension: Redefining South Luzon Connectivity

The Skyway Extension Project serves as the critical "missing link" for motorists traveling between the South Luzon Expressway (SLEX) and the elevated Skyway system. By extending the mainline from the Alabang viaduct to Susana Heights, it bypasses the notorious Alabang bottleneck.

← Back to Home

Project Overview & Scope

The project added approximately 3.9 kilometers of elevated roadway, consisting of three northbound lanes and two southbound lanes. It effectively expands the Skyway’s footprint to reach the southernmost parts of Metro Manila, providing a direct connection for commuters coming from Laguna, Batangas, and Cavite.

Key Engineering Milestones

    Direct Access: Motorists can now access the Skyway from the Soldiers’ Hills area, bypassing the Alabang viaduct entirely.

    Capacity Expansion: The project increased the total capacity of the Skyway by 4,500 vehicles per hour northbound and 3,000 vehicles per hour southbound.

    Seamless Integration: It links directly with Skyway Stage 3, allowing for a continuous elevated trip from Susana Heights in Muntinlupa all the way to Balintawak in Quezon City.

Commuter Essentials

To make the most of this infrastructure, keep these tips in mind:


  • Check Balance via App: Use the Autosweep RFID mobile app or SMS service before heading out.
  • Lane Discipline: The Extension features dedicated lanes for Class 1 vehicles. Ensure you are in the correct lane well before the Alabang fork.
  • Speed Limits: While the road is smoother, the 60 km/h to 80 km/h speed limit is strictly enforced via radar to ensure safety on the elevated curves.

    • Commuter Tips

    Always ensure your Autosweep RFID has sufficient balance before entering the ramp to avoid delays at the sensors. Peak hours remain heavy between 7:00 AM and 9:00 AM.
